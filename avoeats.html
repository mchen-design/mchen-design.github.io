AvoEats

AvoEats is an app designed to use techniques that apply
friction to the online food ordering process in order to
discourage users from making unhealthy choices.

Role

Product Designer, Visual Designer

Tools

Figma, Illustrator

Team

Jiexin(Jessie) Lyu

Mentors

Niloufar Salehi, Tonya Nguyen

Duration

Two weeks (Sep 2019)

Background

This project was the result of one of many two-week design sprints during my Fall 2019 Introduction to HCI Design class at UC Berkeley. Our current class module called for my team to ‚Äúdesign for friction‚Äù through the use of subtle, unintrusive design choices that would discourage users from performing a certain action.

As a two-person team, Jessie and I diverged early on in the project to research ideas for branding and create low fidelity wireframes. At the end of the sprint, we worked together on the high fidelity prototypes in Figma.

Objective

We noticed the prevalence of fast food chain partnerships on popular food delivery apps such as Doordash and Uber Eats, with the apps often featuring these brands on their front pages‚Äîmaking it easy for customers to choose these unhealthy options. As a result, we wanted to use friction to encourage healthy food ordering online and discourage the ordering of fast food.

Solution

Our final deliverable was a high fidelity prototype of our app that showed our implementations of friction into our designs. This included stylistic choices, promotion of healthy menus, and greater caloric transparency.

While Jessie got started on the wireframes, I worked on the visual design for the app.

Moodboard

My first step was to create a moodboard to get a general feel for the colors, themes, and vibes of the topic we were focusing on. A simple Google search of ‚Äúhealthy food‚Äù yielded many images like these:

Overall, there was a lot of green. What caught my eye the most was the popularity of avocados in many of the dishes. I decided to center the branding around the color and shape of an avocado since it reliably seemed to bring a healthy connotation to screens.

Colors

Our color palette was derived directly from the main colors of an avocado.

Logo

Jessie and I came up with a long list of names for our app; among these we selected ‚ÄòAvoEats‚Äô because it was concise and represented our avocado branding concept well. I then worked on different versions of our logo: from wordmarks to symbols, I ended up with a combination logo that used our selected typeface, Rubik, and an original avocado icon.
